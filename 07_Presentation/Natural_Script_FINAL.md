## SLIDE 2 — THE PROBLEM

"So let me start with something that's probably happening
at your company right now.

Most e-commerce brands use something called **last-click attribution.**

It sounds technical — but the idea is simple.
Whoever gets the last click before a purchase... gets all the credit.

And that's almost always a paid ad.

So picture this — `[pause]`
A nano influencer makes a beautiful video.
Someone watches it. Gets inspired. Wants to buy.
They wait three days... then click a retargeting ad... and buy.

**The influencer gets zero credit. The ad gets everything.**

And brands are making real budget decisions based on this.
Right now. Today.

They're cutting the creators that are actually *moving* people —
and they don't even know it."

*[Click → Slide 3]*

## SLIDE 3 — THE DATA

"For this project I worked with two real datasets.

The first — a YouTube influencer dataset.
**4,527 channels.** Nine niches. 82 countries.
This is where all the influencer analysis comes from.

The second — a Facebook A/B test. **588,000 users.**
Randomly assigned to either see a paid ad — or not.

And because it was randomised, `[pause]`
I can actually say the ads *caused* more conversions.
Not just correlated. Caused.

That distinction matters.

**Six hypotheses. All six confirmed.**"

## TABLEAU — H1 FINDING

*[Point to the tier bar chart, top left]*

"— starting with this.

The question here was simple:
do smaller creators actually outperform bigger ones?

The metric I'm using is **VPS — Views Per Subscriber.**
It's total views divided by subscriber count.
It tells you how efficiently a channel turns its followers into actual viewers.

So — `[pause]` — look at the numbers.

Nano channels. Under 10,000 subscribers.
Median VPS of **0.884.**

Mega channels — over one million.
**0.326.**

That's roughly **2.7 times** the efficiency. `[pause]`

And the p-value is 9.21e-263.
That is not a rounding error. That result is statistically certain."

## SLIDE 7 — H3: SIZE VS ENGAGEMENT

"Third hypothesis — and this one reinforces everything we just saw.

As channels grow bigger, does their engagement drop?

Yes. **For every 10× increase in subscriber count,
VPS drops by 41%.** `[pause]`

The reason makes sense when you think about it.
Large channels accumulate passive followers —
people who subscribed years ago and rarely watch anymore.

Smaller creators have something different.
A tighter community. An audience that actually shows up.

That's what makes nano creators so powerful."

*[Click → Slide 8]*

---

## SLIDE 8 — H4: PAID ADS

"Now — I've been talking about influencers.
But what about the moment someone actually *buys*?

That's where paid ads come in.

In a controlled experiment — 588,000 real users,
randomly assigned — paid ads converted **43% better** than organic.

Organic group: 1.79%.
Paid group: **2.55%.** `[pause]`

And because the assignment was random —
this isn't a correlation. This is **cause and effect.**

So here's the complete picture:

Influencers open the door.
Paid ads close the sale.

They're not competing — they're working together.
The mistake is treating them as the same thing
and measuring them the same way."

*[Click → Slide 9]*

## SLIDE 9 — H5: GEOGRAPHY

"A quick geographic layer —

Brazil leads with a median VPS of 1.12.
Turkey and South Africa follow at 0.79 and 0.75.
All well above average.

And when you look at *why* — `[pause]`
these markets are dominated by nano and micro creators
in Fashion, Lifestyle, and Beauty.

The exact same pattern we already found.
Just replicated at country level.

If you're running a global influencer campaign —
these are the markets to prioritise first."

## SLIDE 10 — H6: AD TIMING

"Last hypothesis — `[pause]`
and this one genuinely surprised me.

Does the *timing* of a paid ad affect its conversion rate?

It does. A lot.

Saturday at 5am — **5.68% conversion rate.**
More than double the daily average.

Monday and Tuesday afternoons, 2 to 4pm — consistently strong.

Now — Saturday 5am sounds strange. `[pause]`
But think about *who's* online at that hour.

Someone already browsing with intent.
Not half-asleep scrolling. Actually shopping.

And almost zero ad competition at that time.

Same budget. Same creative.
Just better timing.
And you could more than double your result.

`[pause]`

That's essentially free improvement."

## SLIDE 11 — RECOMMENDATIONS

"So — based on everything — three things I'd actually do.

**One.**
Partner with nano creators in Fashion, Lifestyle, and Beauty.
Not one mega influencer. A *portfolio* of nano creators.
Five times the efficiency. And the data tells you exactly who to target.

**Two.**
Schedule paid ads for Saturday 5 to 7am
and Monday–Tuesday 2 to 4pm.
Highest-converting windows from half a million real users.
Same budget — better results.

**Three.** `[pause]`
Stop using last-click attribution.

Influencers open the door. Paid ads close it.
If you only measure the last click —
you'll systematically undervalue the creator's role
and put your budget in the wrong place.

Measure both channels. For what they *actually* do."

## 💬 Q&A — NATURAL ANSWERS

**"Can you show how tier and niche interact?"**
→ Switch to Tab 1 → point to Sankey → click Nano → flows mostly to High → say: *"Every tier flows somewhere different — but Nano consistently lands on High. That's the visual proof of H1."*

**"R² is 0.27 — that's not very strong, is it?"**
→ *"27% explained by a single variable in complex human behaviour — that's actually meaningful. The other 73% is content quality, community age, posting frequency. Those don't invalidate the finding. They add nuance to it."*

**"How do you scale nano creators?"**
→ *"You don't replace one mega creator with one nano. You build a portfolio. Ten nano creators, 10K subscribers each — that's 100K reach, with 2.7× better engagement per subscriber. The strategy shifts from reach-first to efficiency-first."*

**"Your datasets are from different platforms — can you compare them?"**
→ *"I'm not comparing them directly. YouTube answers how influencers perform. Facebook answers how paid ads convert. Two questions, two datasets, one funnel."*
